# Amazon 6-Pager Template

> Guide for the doc-writer agent. Each section shows its purpose, target length, and what to include.
> Body sections must be written in narrative prose — no bullets. Bullets are only permitted in the Appendix.

---

# [Document Title]

**Date:** [Date]
**Author:** [Name]
**Status:** Draft / Review / Final
**Distribution:** [Team or Org]

---

## Introduction

*Purpose: Set context. Why does this document exist, and why now? State the problem or opportunity.*
*Target: ~0.5 page (2-3 paragraphs)*
*Lead with the most important thing — not background.*

[Write 2-3 paragraphs of narrative prose. Start with the most important context sentence. End with a clear statement of what this document covers and what it is asking the reader to do or decide.]

---

## Goals

*Purpose: Define what success looks like in measurable terms.*
*Target: ~0.5 page*
*Use numbers. "Reduce latency by 40% by Q3" is better than "improve performance". Include timeframes.*

[Write narrative prose describing the goals. Each goal must be specific and measurable. Explain why each goal matters to the customer or the business.]

---

## Tenets

*Purpose: State the guiding principles for this initiative. These are opinionated stances, not corporate values.*
*Target: 3-5 tenets, ~0.5 page total*
*Each tenet is a real constraint that a team member could use to resolve a difficult trade-off decision.*

**Tenet 1: [Name]**
[One paragraph explaining this tenet and the trade-off it encodes. What does it mean in practice? What would violate it?]

**Tenet 2: [Name]**
[One paragraph]

**Tenet 3: [Name]**
[One paragraph]

---

## Current State

*Purpose: Honest assessment of where things stand today. Do not spin this section.*
*Target: ~1 page (3-4 paragraphs)*
*Include real metrics. Acknowledge what is not working. Readers can tell when this section is sanitised — it destroys document credibility.*

[Write 3-4 paragraphs describing the current situation with specific metrics, known issues, and honest assessment of gaps. Cover what is working, what is not, and the magnitude of the problem.]

---

## Lessons Learned

*Purpose: What has the team tried? What worked, what did not, and what was learned?*
*Target: ~1 page (3-4 paragraphs)*
*Be honest about failures. "We tried X and it did not work because Y" is more useful than a list of wins.*

[Write 3-4 paragraphs covering experiments, initiatives, or approaches tried previously. For each, state clearly what was learned. Connect lessons to the path forward — show this document is informed by experience.]

---
