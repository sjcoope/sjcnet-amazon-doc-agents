# 1-Pager Template

> A single-page executive summary used to pitch an idea, request a decision, or brief a senior leader quickly. It must fit on one printed page — approximately 500-700 words of prose.
>
> Every word earns its place. No section should be padded. If you cannot say it in one page, the thinking is not clear enough yet.
>
> All sections are prose only — no bullets.

---

# [Document Title]

**Date:** [Date]
**Author:** [Name]
**Distribution:** [Names or team]

---

## Context

*Purpose: One paragraph. Why is this document being written, and what does the reader need to know to understand the rest?*
*Target: 3-5 sentences*
*Do not restate things the reader already knows. Only include context that is genuinely necessary.*

[Write one tight paragraph setting the scene. End with a sentence that bridges directly into the problem or opportunity below.]

---

## Problem or Opportunity

*Purpose: What is the specific problem or opportunity this document addresses?*
*Target: 3-5 sentences*
*Quantify wherever possible. A problem without a number is an opinion.*

[Write one paragraph. State the problem specifically, with data. Make clear who is affected and to what degree.]

---

## Recommendation

*Purpose: What are you proposing, and why is this the right approach?*
*Target: 4-6 sentences*
*State the recommendation in the first sentence — do not build to it. Then justify it briefly.*

[Write one paragraph. Lead with the recommendation directly. Follow with the 2-3 strongest reasons it is the right call. Acknowledge the most obvious alternative and why you are not recommending it.]

---

## Expected Outcome

*Purpose: What happens if the recommendation is approved and executed well?*
*Target: 2-4 sentences*
*Be specific. Tie the outcome back to the problem stated above.*

[Write one paragraph describing the measurable result. Include a metric or target where possible.]

---

## Ask

*Purpose: What do you need from the reader, right now?*
*Target: 1-3 sentences*
*Be explicit. "Feedback welcome" is not an ask. "Approval to proceed to build" is.*

[One or two sentences stating exactly what you need: a decision, funding, headcount, an introduction, or something else. Include a timeframe if relevant.]
