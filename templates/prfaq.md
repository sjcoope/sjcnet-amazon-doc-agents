# PRFAQ Template (Press Release / FAQ)

> A PRFAQ starts with a mock press release written as if the product or feature has already launched, followed by two FAQ sections. It is the primary Amazon format for defining new products, features, or initiatives by working backwards from the customer.
>
> The press release must be written in past tense as if launch day has already happened. It should be readable by anyone — no jargon, no internal references.
>
> Total length: 1 page (press release) + 2-5 pages (FAQs). The press release is prose only. FAQs use Q&A format.

---

# PRESS RELEASE

## [Headline]

*One line. Product name + the single most important customer benefit. Written as a newspaper headline.*
*Example: "Amazon Introduces Dash Button, Enabling Customers to Reorder Products with a Single Press"*

### [Subheadline]

*One sentence expanding on the headline. Add the key detail that the headline could not fit.*
*Example: "The $4.99 Wi-Fi connected device attaches anywhere in the home and reorders a customer's favourite products automatically."*

**[City], [Date]** — [Opening paragraph. 3-4 sentences. This is the most important paragraph — it must stand alone. Cover: what the product is, who it is for, and the core problem it solves. Write as if a journalist is summarising the announcement.]

[Problem paragraph. 2-3 sentences. Describe the customer problem in vivid, specific terms. Make the reader feel the frustration of the problem before presenting the solution. Do not mention the product here — just the problem.]

[Solution paragraph. 2-3 sentences. Introduce the product and explain how it solves the problem. Be specific about what makes it work. Avoid superlatives and marketing language.]

> "[Executive quote. 2-3 sentences in first person from a named Amazon leader. The quote should convey genuine excitement and explain why Amazon built this. It should sound like a real person, not a press release robot. Name and title go below.]"
>
> — [First Name Last Name], [Title], [Team/Org]

[Customer experience paragraph. 3-4 sentences. Walk through what it is like to actually use this product from the customer's perspective. Be concrete and specific — describe a real moment in a customer's day. Make it feel tangible.]

> "[Customer quote. 2-3 sentences from a named fictional customer. The quote should describe a specific benefit they experienced — not generic praise. Include their name, location, and a brief identifier like 'mother of three' or 'small business owner'.]"
>
> — [Customer Name], [City, State]

[Closing paragraph. 2-3 sentences. Availability, pricing (if applicable), and how customers can get it. End with a forward-looking statement about what this means for customers.]

## Internal FAQs
*Questions a senior leader, engineer, finance partner, or legal reviewer would ask. These can be more technical, strategic, or operational. Aim for 6-10 questions. This is where you address feasibility, risk, cost, and trade-offs honestly.*

**Q: Why are we doing this now? What is the strategic rationale?**
A: [2-4 sentences on market timing, competitive context, or customer demand signals]

**Q: How did we validate that customers actually want this?**
A: [Describe research, signals, or data that informed the decision]

**Q: What does success look like at 6 months, 1 year, and 3 years?**
A: [Specific metrics with targets for each time horizon]

**Q: What is the estimated cost to build and the expected return?**
A: [High-level build cost, operating cost, and projected financial impact]

**Q: What are the top 3 risks and how are we mitigating them?**
A: [List each risk with a specific mitigation approach]

**Q: What does the build plan look like and what teams are involved?**

**Q: What are we explicitly NOT doing with this launch, and why?**
A: [Scope boundaries — what was considered and cut, and the rationale]

A: [Answer]

**Q: [Question about a legal, compliance, or policy consideration]**
A: [Answer]

**Q: [Question a skeptical VP would ask to poke a hole in this]**
A: [Direct, honest answer]
